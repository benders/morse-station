# Component notes — MAX98357A audio amp (Heltec V4 only)

The **MAX98357A** is a digital **I2S-input** class-D amp (~3.2 W into 4 Ω at 5 V)
driving a **4 Ω speaker**. It is the sidetone output **only on the Heltec V4** —
it replaces the earlier PWM-on-GPIO4 → RC low-pass → **PAM8403** analog chain.
The Cardputer ADV still uses its on-board ES8311 codec via M5.Speaker (see
`cardputer-adv.md`), so none of this applies there.

Because the amp takes PCM over I2S, there is no PWM carrier and no RC filter to
fit, and **volume is a true software amplitude** (`sidetone_set_volume`) with
constant timbre at every level — the clean RSSI→loudness gradient the old
PWM-sine path was reaching for.

## Wiring

3-wire I2S plus power. The pin map is defined in `platformio.ini`
(`[env:heltec_v4]` build flags `-DPIN_I2S_BCLK/_LRCLK/_DIN`); `src/pins.h`
consumes those flags (with matching fallbacks). Change pins in `platformio.ini`,
not in code:

| MAX98357A pin | Heltec GPIO | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **BCLK** | 48 | bit clock |
| **LRC**  | 4  | word / left-right select |
| **DIN**  | 47 | serial data |
| **VIN**  | — | **3V3 rail** (runs 2.5–5.5 V; see note below) |
| **GND**  | — | common ground with the Heltec |
| **GAIN** | — | **tie to VIN for 6 dB** (what we run). Do **not** leave it floating — see warning below. Other options: GND = 12 dB, 100 kΩ→GND = 15 dB, 100 kΩ→VIN = 3 dB, floating = 9 dB (avoid) |
| **SD_MODE** | — | **no wire needed** — the breakout's on-board 1 MΩ SD→VIN strap already selects **(L+R)/2** mix and holds the amp out of shutdown |

> **Power off 3V3, not 5 V.** The MAX98357A is happy on 2.5–5.5 V, and 3V3 is
> the only rail present on battery (the 5 V/VBUS rail exists only while USB is
> plugged in), so a battery fox *must* use 3V3. Supply voltage only sets the
> amp's max output power — ~3.2 W into 4 Ω at 5 V vs ~1.3–1.4 W at 3.3 V — and a
> Morse sidetone needs only tens of milliwatts, so 3V3 is already far louder than
> you'll want. If you ever need more level at 3V3, bump the **GAIN** pin (GAIN→GND
> = 12 dB, or 100 kΩ→GND = 15 dB) rather than chasing the 5 V rail.

> **Strap GAIN — never float it.** A floating GAIN pin nominally selects 9 dB,
> but the pin is high-impedance and the level-detect latches off whatever stray
> voltage it floats to. On our first build this produced *intermittent* harsh,
> overdriven tone — clean on some messages, distorted on others, "almost random"
> — that looked like supply sag but was the GAIN strap. Tying **GAIN → VIN (6 dB)**
> made the output stable and clean all the way to `vol 32`. Always strap GAIN to a
> defined level; if you want more loudness use a *defined* higher setting (GND =
> 12 dB, 100 kΩ→GND = 15 dB), not floating.

> GPIO47/48 are S3-only high GPIOs, chosen because they are clear of the radio
> (8–14), FEM (2/5/7/46), OLED (17/18/21), VEXT (36), battery (1/37), the key
> (6), the boot strap (0) and native USB (19/20). **Confirm they're broken out
> on your board revision.** DIN reuses GPIO4 from the retired PWM sidetone.

The firmware sends identical samples on the L and R slots, so the `(L+R)/2`
mix reproduces the tone at full amplitude. The common MAX98357A breakout
already carries a **1 MΩ SD→VIN resistor** (the board schematic's `1.011 MΩ =
LEFT/2 + RIGHT/2` strap), which selects `(L+R)/2` and holds the amp out of
shutdown — so **leave SD unwired**. The earlier "tie SD_MODE high" jumper is
redundant on this breakout. If you prefer to hard-select a single channel you'd
change that strap (the datasheet options are 0 Ω SD→VIN = left, 370 kΩ = right);
`SD` below ~0.16 V is **shutdown** (a hardware mute, if you ever wire it to a
GPIO).

## Sidetone generation

The ESP32-S3 has no true DAC, but the MAX98357A wants digital audio anyway, so
`sidetone.cpp` streams 16-bit PCM over **I2S (`I2S_NUM_0`, 24 kHz)**:

- A dedicated **feeder FreeRTOS task** (pinned to core 0) free-runs the I2S DMA.
  It traces a 256-point sine by DDS at the fixed tone frequency (**750 Hz**,
  `TONE_HZ`), scales each sample by the volume gain, and writes interleaved L/R
  frames. `i2s_write` blocks on DMA space, so the task self-paces.
- **Key down/up ramp a raised-cosine envelope** (~8 ms attack/release) rather
  than hard-gating the tone. Two bugs were fixed in turn here:
  - A **hard cut** steps from a mid-cycle sample straight to zero; the step's
    click energy depends on the random cutoff phase, so some elements sounded
    much louder than others.
  - A **linear** ramp fixes that but still has sharp corners (slope
    discontinuities) at its ends, which splatter spectrally — heard as a
    "chirp"/click, loudest on the first element after a silence (a word start).
  The shipping envelope is therefore a **raised cosine** (`0.5 - 0.5·cos`, a
  precomputed Q15 table), which has zero slope at both endpoints: no corners, no
  splatter, identical loudness on every element. The DDS phase free-runs and the
  clock keeps running through silence, so there's no setup latency on the first
  keyed element.

### Volume curve is exponential

`sidetone_set_volume(0..255)` maps onto the sample amplitude with a **dB-linear
(exponential)** curve, from a faint floor (~ −36 dB) up to full swing, so equal
input steps give equal *perceived* loudness steps. (Currently the hunter leaves
volume at full; the curve is ready if RSSI→loudness is re-enabled.)

## Mute

`sidetone_set_mute()` makes the feeder task emit silence while remembering the
held key state, so unmute resumes a sounding element. It is persisted
(`config::muted`), applied at boot, and toggled by the BLE/serial `mute`
command. (The Cardputer mirrors the same API via `M5.Speaker.stop()`.)
