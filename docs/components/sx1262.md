# Component notes — Semtech SX1262 radio

The SX1262 is the radio on **both** platforms: on-board (behind a FEM) on the
Heltec V4, and on the removable Cap LoRa-1262 on the Cardputer ADV. Driven via
**RadioLib** (`jgromes/RadioLib`) through the thin wrapper in `src/radio.cpp`.

## What it is

- Sub-GHz **(G)FSK / LoRa** transceiver. We use **GFSK** only.
- Chip TX ceiling **+22 dBm**; quoted RX sensitivity ~**−120 dBm** at 4.8 kbps
  GFSK (vs ~−90 dBm for 2.4 GHz ESP-NOW — a ~30 dB link-budget win, before the
  ~8 dB lower free-space path loss at 905 vs 2440 MHz).
- Constant-envelope modulation → the PA stays in compression, no AM distortion.

## Lessons learned

- **No OOK.** The whole SX126x family dropped OOK (the SX127x/RFM69 still have
  it). This design only needs GFSK, so it's a non-issue — but don't plan an OOK
  mode on this part.
- **TCXO voltage is a silent gotcha.** `beginFSK()` takes a `tcxoVoltage` arg.
  Both our boards use a **1.8 V TCXO** (confirmed on HW for the Heltec V4 and the
  Cap LoRa-1262). If a future module uses a **plain crystal**, this must be
  `0.0f` or `beginFSK()` fails with `RADIOLIB_ERR_SPI_CMD_*` / a timeout. When
  bringing up a new LoRa module, if init fails, suspect the TCXO setting first
  (cross-check a Meshtastic variant for that module).
- **DIO2 is the antenna RF switch.** `setDio2AsRfSwitch(true)` lets the chip flip
  the external TX/RX switch automatically. Both boards wire it this way (on the
  Heltec V4.2 it also drives the FEM CTX; the Cap wires DIO2 to its on-cap RF
  switch). The Heltec **V4.3** is the exception — its FEM TX/RX line is a
  software-driven GPIO, not DIO2 (see `heltec-v4.md`).
- **Sync word filters foreign traffic in hardware.** The 2-byte sync word
  `{0x2D, 0xD4}` is matched by the radio; packets without it never reach the MCU,
  cutting RX ISR load. (RadioLib also offers `setNodeAddress` /
  `setBroadcastAddress` hardware address filtering, not currently used.)
- **RSSI is read per packet** (`getRSSI()` after `readData()`), then mapped to
  the hunter's volume/bar. The mapping must be **dB-linear (exponential)** to
  sound right — see `pam8403.md` and the hunter volume notes.
- **One config serves both boards.** The link parameters in `radio.cpp` (905.0
  MHz, 4.8 kbps, 5 kHz dev, 39 kHz RX BW, 16-bit preamble) are device-independent;
  only the SPI pins, TCXO, and FEM presence differ.
